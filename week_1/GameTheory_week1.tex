\noindent $n$ players choose Contribute (C) or Not (N). Good provided iff at least $k$ contribute.
\newline Preferences: free-ride $\succ$ contribute+good succeeds $\succ$ not contribute+ good fails $\succ$ wasted capital.
\newline
\newline Let s players choose Contribute, then we have the following cases

\section*{Case 1: $s > k$ contributors}

Any contributor can switch to N, still good provided ($s-1 \geq k$), and they go from outcome 2 to outcome 1. Strictly better. \textbf{Not a PSNE.}

\section*{Case 2: $s = k$ contributors}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Contributor deviates to N: $s$ drops to $k-1$, good fails, outcome 2 $\to$ 3. No incentive.
  \item Non-contributor deviates to C: outcome 1 $\to$ 2. No incentive.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{PSNE.} There are $\binom{n}{k}$ such equilibria.

\section*{Case 3: $1 \leq s < k$ contributors}

Good not provided. Any contributor gets outcome 4; switching to N gives outcome 3. Strictly better.
\newline \textbf{Not a PSNE.}

\section*{Case 4: $s = 0$ (all N)}

A lone deviator contributes, $s=1 < k$, good still fails, outcome 4 $\to$ 3. No incentive. \textbf{Not a PSNE.}

\section*{Result}

All PSNEs: exactly $k$ contributors $\left(\binom{n}{k} \text{ equilibria}\right)$ or nobody contributes (1 equilibrium). Total: $\binom{n}{k} + 1$.
